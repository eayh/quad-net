Abstract

In recent text-to-speech synthesis and voice conversion systems, a mel-spectrogram is commonly applied as an intermediate representation, and the need for a mel-spectrogram vocoder is increasing. A mel-spectrogram vocoder must solve three inverse problems: recovery of the original-scale magnitude spectrogram, phase reconstruction, and frequency-to-time conversion. In this paper, we propose a lightweight model, Quad-Net, which simplifies this process by integrating an inverse short-time Fourier transform (iSTFT) to reconstruct raw waveforms efficiently, reducing computational overhead while maintaining high speech quality.
